2.What is a zoopraxiscope and how does it relate to web and interaction design? Find another example from filmmaking or another medium that has inspired digital design.

The origin of Zoopraxiscope was a continent horse photograph invented by a British photographer. He has taken a photo of a horse running in every scounde, and printed it on a glass-disc. When the glass-disc is illuminated from behind, it will show the movement of the horse running. The incense called it Zoopraxiscope, and this is one of the origins of the screen too. Zoopraxiscope and web and interaction design were both on screen. Screen was a neature materio, it is in there all the time, which can give the power of giving things a movement, a variability, the author called theis as flux in the article. For example, when you visit the website of Apple, you will find out that the image or text will bring a movement and animation when you scroll up and down, I think that was the meaning of flux.

3. Do you agree with that screens want "flux"? Explain why you do or do not.

I agree that screens need "flux". A screen was a nature matrio, and flux was a developability element of screens, it was controllable and variably way which can be done in the detail design. An over flux may not be a good thing, but an appropriate flux can help describe and simplify the information for the user, which can finish the goal of optimizing user experience.
